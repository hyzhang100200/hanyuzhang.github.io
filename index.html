Hey, this is Hanyu!

I am a doctoral student in Quantitative Marketing at the Goizueta Business School, Emory University.

My research focuses on the role of social network structure in consumer interactions, with specific applications to mobile locational targeting, platform growth, consumer churn/engagement, and community sociology. By delving into the dynamics of network structures, I aim to explore how these network structures influence the flow of information across network ties, as well as consumer preferences and behaviors.

I utilize a wide range of methodologies, spanning from causal inference using quasi-experimental methods to machine learning techniques.
